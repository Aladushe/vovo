About the club

history of organization

professional basketball team in the Pacific Division of the Western Conference of the National Basketball Association. The club was founded in 1970 in Buffalo, New York and was called the Buffalo Braves. In 1978, the team moved to San Diego and changed its name to the San Diego Clippers. In 1984, the club moved to Los Angeles, where they are based today, playing home games at the Staples Center arena.
